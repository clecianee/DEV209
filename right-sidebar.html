ANNETTE LAKE

Nature & Scenery

The trail starts through dense forests of Douglas fir, cedar, and hemlock and follows Humpback Creek early on, including a scenic waterfall near the start. After climbing out of the woods, you'll pass open slopes and talus fields with mountain views and wildflowers in spring/summer. The destination is Annette Lake, an alpine lake framed by peaks like Abiel and Silver Peak — ideal for a rest, photos, or (brave) swim in clear, cold water. There are campsites near the lake if you want an overnight trip.

Best Season to Hike

Late Spring through Early Fall (June-October) is the best and most popular window — snow mostly melts, wildflowers bloom, and the lake is accessible with fewer hazards. Winter hiking/snowshoeing is possible with proper gear, but avalanche chutes and snowy sections can make the route more challenging and require winter experience and permits (Sno-Park pass).

Tips

You'll need a Northwest Forest Pass for parking during summer months and a Sno-Park permit for winter parking. Trail can be muddy early in the season and rough road conditions to the trailhead mean driving slowly. Start early on weekends to avoid crowds — this is a popular hike near Seattle.

Trail Overview

Distance: ~7 - 7.5 miles round-trip (≈11-12 km).

Elevation Gain: ~1,800-2,000 ft (≈550-610 m).

Difficulty: Moderate — steady elevation gain with some switchbacks; not overly steep but sustained.

Average Time to Complete: ~4 - 5 hours total as a day hike.
