SNOW LAKE

Trail Overview

Distance: Approximately 6.6-7.2 miles round trip (~10.6-11.6 km) out-and-back to the lakeshore.

Elevation Gain: About 1,600-1,800 feet (~490-550 m).

Difficulty: Moderate to moderately strenuous — sustained uphill with some steeper sections as you approach the lake basin.

Average Time to Complete: ~3.5 - 5 hours depending on pace, breaks, and time spent at the lake.

Nature & Scenery

The hike starts at the Alpental Ski Area parking lot and ascends through shaded forest with views of the South Fork Snoqualmie valley. Above the tree line the trail opens to alpine terrain, with rocky areas, wildflowers in summer, and striking views of Chair Peak and Kaleetan Peak. Snow Lake itself sits in a wide mountain basin — a stunning alpine lake fed by snowmelt and surrounded by rugged peaks. The trail offers scenic viewpoints before descending slightly into the lake basin.

Best Season to Hike

Summer to Early Fall (July-October) is the most common and safest season for this hike: trails are usually snow-free, water flow is lower, and weather is more stable. Early season (Late Spring to mid-July) can still have snow on the trail, especially near the saddle and lake basin, and requires care. Winter snowshoeing is possible but avalanche risk is significant without experience and proper gear — generally not recommended for beginners in snowy conditions.

Tips

A Northwest Forest Pass is required for parking at the trailhead. The trail is very popular and parking fills early on summer weekends — arrive early or go on a weekday if possible. Good hiking boots and plenty of water are recommended — the trail is rocky in places with roots and occasional steep pitches near the lake.
